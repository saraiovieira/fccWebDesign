Vicent Van Gogh

One of the greatest Post-Impressionists painters

“I want to touch people with my art. I want them to say 'he feels deeply, he feels tenderly'.”
― Vincent Willem van Gogh


Here's a timeline of Van Gogh's life:

  • 1853 - On 30 March, Vincent Willem van Gogh was born in Groot-Zundert, Netherlands. He was the eldest son of the Protestant clergyman Theodorus van Gogh and Anna Cornelia Carbentus
  • 1864 - He was sent to boarding school in Zevenbergen
  • 1869- Van Gogh is employed by the international art dealer Goupil & Co. in the Hague gallery
  • 1873 - Van Gogh was transferred to the company branch in London. This was a happy period for Van Gogh: he was having success at work and at twenty years of age he earned more than his father.
  • 1875 - Against his will, he was posted permanendy to Paris. His aversion to the art trade grew steadily and a year later he quit the company
  • 1876 - Van Gogh devoted his life to the evangelization of the poor in the town of Borinage, Belgium
  • 1880 - Van Gogh moved to Brussels and began to learn the skills needed to become an artist
  • 1885 - Completion of The Potato Eaters
  • 1886 - Van Gogh moved to Paris. Discovered Impressionists and Post Impressionists
  • 1887 - Completion of Self Portrait with Straw Hat
  • 1888 - Van Gogh moved to Arles to create a school of art. There he painted the famous sunflower pieces and began to suffer from mental problems. In September, he painted Starry Night Over the Rhone and The Café Terrace on the Place du Forum, Arles, at Night. In October he painted Vincent's Bedroom in Arles and in December, Van Gogh cut off a portion of his ear and commited himself to a mental asylum in Saint Rémy
  • 1889 - He painted Irises and Starry Night
  • 1890 - In February, he painted Almond Blossom. In May, Van Gogh left Saint Rémy, contacted his brother Theo and leaved the asylum to begin care in Auvers-sur-Oise. In July, Vincent Van Gogh shot himself in the chest. He was buried on July 30 at Auvers-sur-Oise
